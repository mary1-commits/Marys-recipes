Mary's Recipes!

Welcome to My Somali Food Blog!

Hi, I'm Maryan! As someone deeply connected to my Somali heritage, I started this blog to share my love for the rich, aromatic, and diverse flavors of Somali cuisine. Growing up, food was more than just nourishment—it was a way to celebrate traditions, bring family together, and tell stories of resilience and history.

Why Somali Food?

Somali cuisine is a beautiful fusion of influences from the Middle East, India, and East Africa. Whether it’s the fragrant rice dishes, slow-cooked meats, or the comforting sweetness of shaah (Somali spiced tea), every meal carries a deep cultural significance. The balance of spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cumin creates flavors that are both bold and comforting.

What You'll Find Here

This blog is my space to explore and document traditional Somali recipes, family favorites, and modern takes on classic dishes. From the beloved Bariis Iskukaris (Somali rice) to the soft, spongy Canjeero, my goal is to preserve and celebrate our culinary heritage.

Join Me on This Journey!

Whether you’re Somali and looking for familiar flavors or new to Somali cuisine and eager to try something different, I hope this blog inspires you to cook, share, and enjoy. Let’s keep the traditions alive—one meal at a time!

Thanks for being here, and I hope you enjoy the journey!
